Before you start to develop your own DNN, make sure that...

... you have .jpg images. You can convert (and resize) your images.
In imageJ: click process > batch > convert and select output format 'JPEG' and set scale factor depending on your original image size.
... your images are approximately 1000 px on 1000 px size. I worked with square-shaped images.
If you have rectangular images, please contact me to make an update to the software.

How to train your own Deep Neural Network (DNN)?

Step 1: Choose sample name and select method "annotate".
Step 2: Choose region of interest: "full image".
Step 3: Fill in other settings such as resolution and image width.
Step 4: Click on select images for mica and select one transmitted light image.
Step 5: Annotate all tracks using left mouse button and proceed. You will receive a zip file with a txt file and jpg file.
Step 6: Repeat step 1 to 5 for ~50-100 images with different track densities.
Contact the developer (Simon) and let him train the DNN or follow the instructions below
Step 7: Make a zip-file with all txt, jpeg files. Add one file 'classes.txt' with content: "Track"
Step 8: Remove all "" in the txt files (2 per txt file).
Step 9: Upload the files to your Google Drive, open Jupyter notebook in in Google Colab and execute all.
Step 10: Contact Simon (Simon.Nachtergaele@UGent.be) to upload the DNN to AI-Track-tive.
